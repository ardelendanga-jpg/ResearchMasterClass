# Newlands Clinic Research Masterclass for Clinicians
**Foundations in Epidemiology, Statistics, and Scientific Writing**

Welcome to the repository for the 12-week intensive online training programme designed to empower clinicians to transition from clinical observers to lead researchers. This course builds a robust foundation in epidemiology, statistics, and scientific data analysis.

## Course Overview
Upon completion of this programme, participants will achieve the following core competencies:
1. Mastery of Research Design and Epidemiology
2. Applied Statistical Literacy
3. Practical Data Proficiency (R Programming)

**Final Impact:** Graduates will possess the end-to-end skills required to conceptualise a clinical study, descriptively analyse data with precision, and confidently contribute evidence-based findings to medical literature.

## Course Structure and Time Commitment
This 12-week programme requires a total typical working time of 84 hours, broken down as follows:

| Course Component | Total Hours |
| :--- | :--- |
| Live workshop sessions (2-3pm, ~1 hour/week) | 12 |
| Post-workshop tasks: Weekly Quizzes & Exercises (~1 hour/week) | 12 |
| Prework content study (~2 hours/week) | 24 |
| Independent work on the Final Presentation Project | 36 |

## Repository Contents
The course materials are divided into three main modules:

* **Module 1: Fundamentals of Epidemiology:** Master the foundational principles of population health research, formulate research questions, design appropriate studies, and calculate key epidemiological measures.
* **Module 2: Statistics for Epidemiology:** Develop statistical literacy to analyse research data, interpret published findings, choose appropriate tests, and understand p-values and confidence intervals.
* **Module 3: Introduction to Data Analysis:** Develop hands-on proficiency in descriptive statistics using R.

## Learning Outcomes
**Epidemiology**
* Formulate clear research questions
* Select appropriate study designs
* Calculate disease measures studies

**Statistics**
* Describe data appropriately
* Choose correct statistical tests
* Interpret results accurately

**Data Analysis**
* Data preparation
* Numerical summaries
* Data presentation

## Grading & Assessment
To successfully pass the course, participants must achieve a **Passing Grade of 70%**. The final grade is weighted as follows:

* **Weekly Quizzes & Exercises:** 30%
* **Participation & Engagement:** 30%
* **Final Presentation Project:** 40%
